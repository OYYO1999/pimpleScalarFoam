# pimpleScalarFoam

Solver DescriptionpimpleScalarFoam is a custom, transient, incompressible fluid dynamics solver developed within the OpenFOAM framework. It is fundamentally extended from the standard pimpleFoam solver, which utilizes the PIMPLE (merged PISO-SIMPLE) algorithm to resolve the pressure-velocity coupling.This customized solver is specifically designed for Direct Numerical Simulation (DNS) and Large Eddy Simulation (LES) of forced convection in wall-bounded turbulent flows. Beyond solving the standard Navier-Stokes equations, pimpleScalarFoam simultaneously resolves a strictly coupled advection-diffusion equation for a passive scalar field (representing temperature, $T$). To accurately capture the fully developed thermal boundary layer without physical domain expansion, a customized volumetric heat source term—strictly proportional to the local streamwise velocity—is directly hard-coded into the scalar matrix. This explicit integration bypasses the computational overhead and potential data-fetching instabilities associated with the runtime fvOptions library, ensuring absolute matrix-level control, stringent energy conservation, and highly optimized parallel computing performance.Governing EquationsThe flow and heat transfer are governed by the unsteady, incompressible Navier-Stokes equations and the passive scalar transport equation.1. Continuity Equation (Mass Conservation):$$\nabla \cdot \mathbf{U} = 0$$2. Momentum Equation (Navier-Stokes):$$\frac{\partial \mathbf{U}}{\partial t} + \nabla \cdot (\mathbf{U} \otimes \mathbf{U}) = -\nabla p + \nu \nabla^2 \mathbf{U} + \mathbf{f}$$3. Passive Scalar Transport Equation (Energy Conservation):$$\frac{\partial T}{\partial t} + \nabla \cdot (\mathbf{U} T) = D \nabla^2 T + S_T$$Where:$\mathbf{U}$ is the velocity vector field.$p$ is the kinematic pressure (pressure divided by the fluid density, $P/\rho$).$\nu$ is the kinematic viscosity of the fluid.$\mathbf{f}$ represents external body forces or artificial momentum source terms (e.g., the constant mean pressure gradient used to drive the channel flow, applied via vectorSemiImplicitSource).$T$ is the passive scalar field representing the fluid temperature ($T_{passive}$).$D$ is the molecular thermal diffusivity, defined as $D = \nu / Pr$, where $Pr$ is the Prandtl number.$S_T$ is the customized volumetric heat source term. In this specific solver configuration for a fully developed channel flow with a constant axial wall heat flux, the source term is explicitly defined as $S_T = \gamma U_x$, where $\gamma = \partial T_w / \partial x$ is the uniform streamwise temperature gradient, and $U_x$ is the instantaneous local streamwise velocity.
